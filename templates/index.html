Who We Are

We are a group of Cambridge postgraduate students working on sensor technologies for healthy and sustainable future.

Cyanovision started out as a summer-long group project aiming to tackle the challenge of timely blue detection of cyanobacteria in water samples.

The Problem

Cyanobacteria can have toxic effects on people, animals and the environment.

What are cyanobacteria?

Cyanobacteria, also known as blue-green algae, are photosynthetic bacteria living on water surfaces and usually present in relatively low concentrations. Cyanobacteria growth can be promoted by hot waters as well as human activities such as the use of fertilisers in agriculture that are rich in nutrients like nitrogen and phosphorus. Rapid multiplication and increase of cyanobacteria in water can result in algal blooms.

Why can cyanobacterial algal blooms be harmful?

The algal blooms often produce toxins detrimental to the health of humans, animals and ecosystems. Blooms can also affect other organisms in the water by blocking sunlight below the surface and preventing their access to oxygen and nutrients. Toxic cyanobacteria blooms have been reported in the UK, for example in Milton Country Park, near Cambridge, in August 2022.

Our Solution

We have created a low-cost solution for cyanobacteria and algae detection in water samples.

Novel microscopy system.

We have created a novel microscopy system based on the OpenFlexure Microscope for the detection of cyanobacteria and algae in fresh water, currently using bright-field microscopy.

Machine Learning-based cyanobacteria detection system.

Our machine learning algorithm, based on objected detection, has been trained to identify cyanobacteria species of different morphologies (e.g. unicellular, filamentous and rod-shaped) as well as the common algae in bright-field images.

User-friendly UI.

We have created a user-friendly UI that enables the use of our software without requiring any coding knowledge

Features

The benefits of timely toxic cyanobacteria and algae detection in lakes and rivers.

Real-time monitoring

Continuous monitoring of toxic bacteria concentrations prevents harm to people and animals.

Microfluidics channel system

Our microscope is connected to a microfluidics channel to filter out larger microorganisms and contaminants as well as ensure the flow of the water is suitable for the imaging
